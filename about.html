I'm a [grade] student with a long-standing obsession with two things that probably shouldn't go together: medicine and storytelling. The Differential is what happens when you combine them.

I've been doing competitive math since first grade — Math Kangaroo, TMSCA all through middle school — which gave me a weird appreciation for problems that have real stakes. Medicine felt like the same thing, just with higher ones. HOSA pulled me in deeper, and somewhere along the way I started wondering why medical education had to be so passive.

I've moved around a lot across the US, which means I've had to get good at starting over and figuring things out independently. This project is one of those things. I built it mostly from scratch, learned what I needed to as I went, and I'm still going.

The Differential is a passion project — not affiliated with any school, organization, or medical institution. Every case is researched carefully, but this is for learning and curiosity, not clinical guidance.
